Argentina 3–0 Croatia

2022 FIFA World Cup | Semi-Final | 2022.12.13

⚽ Lionel Messi 34’ (P)
⚽ Julián Álvarez 39’
⚽ Julián Álvarez 69’

Introduction

Argentina entered this semi-final seeking control through balanced possession and compact defensive structure. Croatia, with their elite trio in midfield, aimed to dictate tempo and wear opponents down through circulation and stability. The match turned on Argentina’s transitional efficiency, pressing triggers, and explosive attacking runs. Croatia dictated possession early but lacked penetration, while Argentina executed decisive counter-attacks and structural changes to close out the game professionally.

Performance Indicators

Possession:
Croatia kept more of the ball, especially in the opening 30 minutes and after falling behind. Argentina accepted long defensive phases and waited for counter-attacking windows.

Chance Creation:
Croatia struggled to create high-value chances from controlled build-up despite midfield superiority. Argentina generated their best opportunities through direct vertical attacks and running behind the back line.

Shape Changes:
Argentina moved between a 4-4-2 mid-block, 4-3-3 build, and a late 5-3-2 to protect the lead. Croatia used a 4-3-3 with Modrić, Kovačić and Brozović controlling possession, then shifted to more attacking structures chasing the game.

Match Analysis
In Possession: Argentina

Central overloads and vertical access
Argentina built with Enzo Fernández and Rodrigo De Paul in wide central roles, while Paredes acted as pivot. Messi drifted between lines, constantly receiving between midfield and defence. Full-backs pushed high, giving width while midfielders created triangles inside.

Transition threat
The breakthrough came from direct attacking sequences rather than slow build. Álvarez’s early penalty win set the tone: Argentina weren’t winning by grinding possession, but by explosive moments. Minutes later, Álvarez ran through three defenders to finish a transition after a Croatian set-piece.

Control through structural switch
After going 2–0 up, Argentina shifted to deeper positions, absorbing pressure and attacking wide spaces vacated by Croatian full-backs. Substitutions allowed a move to a back five, stabilising central defence and neutralising Croatian midfield rotations.

Final blow
Messi’s individual dribble on the right flank, beating Gvardiol before cutting back for Álvarez, illustrated Argentina’s combination of individual quality and tactical clarity — a direct sequence that finished the contest.

In Possession: Croatia

Midfield dominance without penetration
Croatia circulated possession well, with Modrić dropping, Kovačić progressing the ball, and Brozović maintaining control. However, this control lacked a final-third option capable of breaking compact defending.

Wide occupation, low return
Croatia attempted to build in wide channels with advanced full-backs, but Argentina denied central access. Most Croatian attacks ended in low-percentage crosses or blocked vertical passes.

Chasing game problems
At 2–0 down, Croatia attempted structural changes: moving midfielders into higher positions, using wingers inside, and pushing full-backs even deeper forward. Argentina’s low block forced Croatia away from dangerous central zones, reducing clear chances.

One-dimensional late phase
Croatia’s approach became predictable — long spells of sterile possession. Despite controlling territory, they rarely threatened Martínez in goal.

Defensive Organisation

Argentina:
Compact, layered defending through midfield. Distances between lines were tight. Pressing triggers often came from central targets receiving with their backs to goal, forcing turnovers and launching counters.

Croatia:
Early defending focused on limiting Messi’s central reception. Once chasing the game, Croatia became stretched, leaving spaces for Álvarez and midfield runners to attack transitions.

Game-State Management

Argentina dominated the big moments: the penalty, the transition for the second goal, and the structural shift that neutralised Croatia in the second half.

Croatia controlled possession but failed to accelerate the tempo or destabilise Argentina’s low block. Once behind, Croatia lacked an alternative to possession cycling and struggled to break Argentina’s shape.

Discussion

This semi-final emphasised that control without threat means very little at knockout level. Croatia’s midfield dictated rhythm but didn’t create decisive actions. Argentina, meanwhile, managed game states with maturity, switching structures, and striking clinically when opportunities arrived.

Finishing, tactical flexibility, and transitional precision separated the teams.

Conclusion

Argentina reached the World Cup final through tactical intelligence, defensive discipline, and lethal counter-attacking. Croatia’s controlled possession couldn’t convert into meaningful chances, while Argentina dominated critical phases to secure a 3–0 victory.
